The ocean is
The lifeblood of Earth.

David Attenborough

Biologist David Attenborough’s quote, “The ocean is the lifeblood of Earth.” perfectly captures what UN Sustainable Development Goal 14 - Life Below Water is all about. Every living thing on Earth is connected to the ocean.

Yet, our oceans are struggling under the weight of pollution, overfishing, and climate change. Attenborough’s message is a wake-up call for everyone: Don’t litter the ocean.

Deadly
Seigaiha

SDG14 | Life Below Water

Concept

We draw inspiration from the circle and its ordered arrangement into a ripple shape, using outlines with no fill. It represents the negative impact of human activity on the ocean - specifically littering. The ocean surface creates small wave frequencies as the aftermath of these actions.

Concept

I take great inspiration from the ocean and the Japanese wave pattern Seigaiha. In this case, most waves are normal, but some dangerous red ones show what human activity is pouring into the ocean - chemicals, oil, and fertilizers.

Concept

The Seigaiha pattern represents peacefulness and long-lasting life. From this, I want to create a contrast with human destruction to highlight the unhealthy, dangerous, and urgent situation of the globe in modern life. The sun symbol in the center tells the story of order within a system and the dependencies between its elements. We need the ocean to survive, and so does it.

Beautiful ocean is what gives us life, we need to protect it.
